AND
"AND" narrows your search results. If searching for AIDS AND disease you are searching for the word AIDS in the same document as the word disease. The search engine discards all documents except those that have all of the search terms. If just one of these words are missing, the document will not be included in the results.

OR
"OR" broadens your search results. A search for canine OR dog requires that a document have either, one, or both of these terms. This is useful where two different words have similar meanings and are used interchangeably (known as synonyms) as well as broadening the search to include pluralization or addressing various spellings of a word. If just one (at a minumum) of these words appears in a document, the document will be listed in the results.

( ) Parentheses
"( )" are used to group boolean statements, affecting the order of query processing. The statement within a set of parenthesis are treated as a single unit and get processed before any statements outside the parenthesis get processed.

Field1 = 'project' AND (Field2 = 'manager' OR Field2 = 'supervisor')

Retrieves documents with the word "project" in Field1 AND either of the words "manager" or "supervisor" in Field2. The Field2 statement is processed first, then these results are compared to the Field1 statement

(Field1 = 'project' AND Field2 = 'manager') OR Field2 = 'supervisor'

Retrieves documents containing Field1 = "project" AND Field2 = "manager" OR just those having the Field2 term "supervisor" (regardless of data in the other fields). The statement within the parenthesis is processed first, and these results are then compared to the Field2 statement.


Example 1

Field1 contains 'AIDS' AND 'disease'

If you enter this query, you will retrieve a list of all documents which contain both AIDS AND disease in Field1. This is good for narrowing your search to exclude less desirable information. (In the above example, excluded documents may be ones discussing topics such as band aids, first aid, financial aid, teacher aids, etc.).


Example 2

Field1 contains 'dog' OR 'canine' OR 'puppy' OR 'puppies'

If you enter this query, you will retrieve a list of all documents which contain either dog OR canine OR puppy OR puppies in Field1. The use of synonyms is a good technique to help broaden your results.


Example 3

Field1 contains 'tennis' AND (Field1 contains 'racquet' OR 'racket')

If you enter this query, you will retrieve a list of all documents which contain both tennis AND either racquet OR racket in addition.


Example 4

Field1 contains 'television' OR (Field1 contains 'cathode' AND 'tube')

This query would return documents containing either the word television OR both the words cathode AND tube.
